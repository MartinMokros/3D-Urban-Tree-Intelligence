# Sites

The Bratislava implementation testbed will use selected urban tree sites representing different real-world monitoring situations, including street trees, park trees, tree groups, complex crowns, vegetation near buildings, and locations with direct management relevance.

The map below shows the current site planning layer for the hackathon.

## What we aim to capture

<div class="card-grid">
  <div class="card">
    <h3>Street trees</h3>
    <p>Trees along roads and pavements, including narrow spaces, parked cars, buildings, traffic, and other common urban constraints.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Park trees</h3>
    <p>Open-grown trees and park settings where crown structure, canopy form, and full-tree geometry can be assessed more clearly.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Complex vegetation</h3>
    <p>Tree groups, understory, shrubs, overlapping crowns, and situations where segmentation and tree matching become more difficult.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h3>Management relevance</h3>
    <p>Sites selected not only for technical interest, but also for their relevance to practical urban tree monitoring and future implementation.</p>
  </div>
</div>
